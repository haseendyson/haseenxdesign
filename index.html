About & Philosophy

Design as a moral act

My work begins with empathy and ends with action. Trained as an architect and a designer, shaped by India's healthcare inequities and sharpened by time at UC Berkeley and IIT Kanpur, I believe that the best design is invisible — it simply makes life more dignified. I am a Fulbright-Nehru Fellow, Prime Minister Research Fellow, and FARE Fellow. Currently, I serve as Assistant Professor at IIT Hyderabad and hold a visiting collaboration with Imperial College London.

Research Philosophy

Knowledge should travel from the field to the clinic to the classroom and back. I work with communities first, and publish second — privileging the problem over the paper.

Teaching Philosophy

Design is learned by doing, unlearned by observing, and mastered by failing gracefully. I create studio environments where students encounter real constraints and real people.

Design Philosophy

More-than-human centered design. Technology should be frugal, contextual, and kind — especially when it enters spaces as intimate as health and care.

Research

Active threads

01

Mobile Primary Healthcare

Designing deployable, frugal mobile Primary Health Centers (mPHC) for low-resource settings in rural India. From origami-inspired structures to interior ergonomics and wayfinding.

02

XR in Healthcare Education

Developing augmented and virtual reality frameworks for authentic clinical education. Currently piloting with CVD-Diabetes patients in the UK with Loughborough University.

03

Brain–Computer Interfaces

Exploring non-invasive BCIs as interaction channels for assistive and rehabilitative design — where neuroscience meets industrial design.

04

Behavioral & Critical Design

Applying behavioral science to the design of health systems, community health workers' tools (ASHA bags, frontline kits), and stress-reduction products.

05

Medical Device Innovation

Awarded PMECRG grant (₹70L) by ANRF, DST for a new medical device project. Working at the intersection of engineering design and clinical validation.

06

Frugal & Social Innovation

Learning from grassroots jugaad and Mohalla Clinics to inform scalable, equitable health infrastructure models for the Global South.

New ideas I'm exploring

On the horizon

Biometric-Driven Adaptive Learning Environments

Can real-time physiological signals reshape how a simulation responds to a learner? Exploring adaptive XR spaces that shift difficulty, pacing, and content based on stress and attention markers.

Beyond Technological Solutionism in Health Design

A critical design lens on when XR and AI actually help patients — and when they add friction. Presented at CHI '25 Yokohama; developing into a fuller research program.

Deployable Architecture for Emergency Health Response

Post-disaster, post-pandemic, and climate-vulnerable communities need health facilities that can be carried, assembled, and trusted. Connecting origami, material science, and health policy.

Design Canvases as Decision Tools

Translating participatory design methods into lightweight visual canvases that non-designers (clinicians, policymakers, frontline workers) can use independently in the field.
